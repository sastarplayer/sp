By SA STARPLAYER Saturday July-18, 08:00 AM
  Mdunge Skhumbuzo

Al Ahly has defeated Kaizer Chiefs 3-0 to win the Caf Champions League final in Morocco.


While Kaizer Chiefs were hoping to clinch their first Star, Al Ahly took it for the 10th time, with Pitso in charge for two Stars that he won back-to-back last year and this year in the 2020/2021 season.


Pisto said after the game, he had mixed feelings for conquering a team that he grew up supporting.


" I feel conflicted because first I played against a team from my country, and second I played against a team that I supported as a youngster. My history with Kaizer Chiefs as a younger star is massive because my uncle played for Kaizer Chiefs, Doctor Khumalo, used to take me with his car to the games and training sessions of Kaizer Chiefs, and I grew up in the same street with the Chairman, Kaizer Motaung."


The Kaizer Chiefs coach, Stuart Baxter, said the red card of Happy Mashiane brought an advantage to Al Ahly.


" We're massively disappointed. The players in the change room are massively disappointed. We're disappointed not with the workload but in certain situations, the quality of our play was not good enough, and with the 10th man on the field, it was a big mountain to climb."


In an enclosure, Pitso said, Africans should trust, their own people. "I would like to dedicate the trophy to captain Bibo and Mr. Khatib, who believe in me that I could change the fortunes. Before I came, Aly had never won for the past 13 years. He could have gone to Europe, while we can do it ourselves. Even the awards of FIFA, coach of the year, and Ballon' d'Or we don't feature, but I've won trophies."
